# Puppies: A Small Collection

A handful of poems about the smallest, loudest, softest creatures
to ever ruin a pair of slippers.

---

## 1. First Morning Home

*(Free verse)*

She does not know the names of things yet —
not *floor*, not *stairs*, not *mine*.

The kitchen tile is a continent
and her paws are four bewildered explorers
sliding in four different directions.

She finds a sock.
She finds a shoe.
She finds her own tail
and is, briefly, betrayed.

When she falls asleep
it is mid-step,
nose against my ankle,
breath so small
I have to lean down
just to be sure
she is still here.

## 2. Three Haiku for a Sleeping Pup

*(Haiku sequence)*

Belly to the sun —
one ear flicks at a far bird
she will never catch.

Soft snoring, like rain
falling on a paper roof —
the whole house listens.

Twitching paws — she runs
through a field that is not here.
Run, small one. Run well.

## 3. The Zoomies

*(Rhyming, sing-song)*

At 8 PM she's calm and sweet,
a velvet puddle at my feet.
At 8:01 her eyes go wide —
a demon rises from inside.

She tears the rug, she rounds the chair,
she launches eight feet through the air,
she barks at nothing, bites the broom,
she is a hurricane in one small room.

I watch in awe, I watch in dread,
I watch her body, mind, and head
become a single howling ball —
then *thump*, she sleeps. That's it. That's all.

## 4. Things She Has Eaten That She Should Not Have

*(List poem)*

A receipt for groceries.
The corner of a library book (overdue).
One AirPod, recovered.
One AirPod, never spoken of again.
A leaf shaped like Italy.
The tag off the couch.
A second leaf, same tree, different shape, equally suspicious.
Half of a crayon — *Cerulean*.
The word "no," apparently, every time I say it.

## 5. Loyalty

*(Sonnet, loose)*

You do not know the shape of my whole life.
You do not know which days were good or hard,
which calls I dread, which silences are knife,
which doors I close, which gardens I have scarred.

You know the door. You know the sound of keys.
You know my coat, my voice, the way I kneel.
You know my hand. You know my bended knees.
You know what loving someone ought to feel.

And so you wait. Each hour, in your way,
ear to the wood, eye to the narrow crack,
believing — with no proof — I'll come someday,
and treating every coming-back as back.

Whatever else my small, strange life has been,
some creature thought it worth the listening-in.

## 6. Growth

*(Free verse, short)*

Last week the collar fit.
This week it does not.

Last week she fit beneath the chair.
This week she gets stuck halfway,
looks up at me,
furious and embarrassed
and still, somehow,
expecting to be rescued.

She is becoming a dog.
I am becoming
the person she'll remember
when she is old.

We are both,
quietly,
in over our heads.

## 7. Lullaby for a Tired Pup

*(Rhyming, soft)*

The day was long, the grass was wet,
you chased a bee you didn't get.
You barked the mailman down the lane,
you ran outside, you ran back in again.

You drank the puddle, dropped the ball,
you tried to climb the garden wall.
You met a leaf. You met a snail.
You wagged your whole entire tail.

Now close your eyes, my brave small friend,
the day has reached its happy end.
The world is wide, the world is deep —
but here, with me, it's time to sleep.
